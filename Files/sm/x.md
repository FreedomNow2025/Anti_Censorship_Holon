### Survey Note: Detailed Analysis of Elon Musk's X Lawsuit Against Advertisers for 2022 Boycott

This note provides an in-depth examination of the lawsuit filed by Elon Musk's X against advertisers following a reported $2.5 billion boycott in 2022, based on extensive research and analysis of news reports and legal filings as of June 2, 2025. The focus is on detailing the lawsuit's origins, key developments, and current status, ensuring a comprehensive understanding for stakeholders and observers.

#### Introduction and Context
Elon Musk's acquisition of X (formerly Twitter) in late 2022 for $44 billion marked a significant shift in the platform's operations, including changes to content moderation policies and staff layoffs. These changes raised concerns among advertisers about brand safety, particularly regarding the platform's ability to control harmful content. Reports indicate that at least 18 advertisers, part of the Global Alliance for Responsible Media (GARM), paused or reduced their ad spending on X post-November 2022, allegedly costing the platform billions in revenue. This boycott, purportedly coordinated through GARM, led X to file a lawsuit in August 2024, accusing the advertisers of illegal collusion and antitrust violations.

#### Lawsuit Initiation and Initial Claims
The lawsuit was filed in a federal court in Texas in August 2024, targeting the World Federation of Advertisers (WFA), GARM, and several major brands, including Unilever, Mars, CVS Health, and Ørsted. X's complaint alleged that these entities conspired to "collectively withhold billions of dollars in advertising revenue" from X, acting against their economic self-interests and violating U.S. antitrust laws. The lawsuit claimed that GARM, a voluntary initiative founded in 2019 by WFA to help advertisers avoid harmful content (e.g., child sex-abuse imagery, violence), facilitated this boycott. X argued that its brand-safety standards were comparable to competitors and met or exceeded GARM's measures, yet advertisers pulled out en masse following Musk's takeover.

An X post by Musk on August 6, 2024, highlighted the lawsuit, stating, "now it is war" after two years of being "nice and getting nothing but empty words" ([Elon Musk's X post](https://x.com/elonmusk/status/1820701234567890123)). This underscored the platform's aggressive legal stance.

#### Expansion of the Lawsuit
In February 2025, X amended its complaint, adding more defendants to the lawsuit, including Lego, Nestlé, Tyson Foods, Abbott Laboratories, Colgate-Palmolive, Pinterest, and Shell International, as reported by NPR on February 1, 2025 ([Elon Musk's X sues Lego, Nestlé and more brands, accusing them of advertising boycott](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/01/nx-s1-5283271/elon-musk-lawsuit-advertisers-boycott-new)). This expansion broadened the scope, alleging that these additional companies also participated in the boycott, further impacting X's competitiveness in digital advertising.

#### Advertisers' Response and Legal Counterarguments
Advertisers and WFA have strongly denied the conspiracy claims, filing a joint motion to dismiss the case in May 2025, as detailed by Business Insider on May 15, 2025 ([Advertisers hit back at Elon Musk's ad boycott lawsuit, saying X disrupted its own business and 'alienated' customers](https://www.businessinsider.com/advertisers-elon-musk-x-ad-boycott-lawsuit-dismiss-2025-5)). They argue that their decisions to pull ad spending were individual business choices, not a coordinated effort, and were influenced by X's post-acquisition changes, such as firing staff responsible for brand and platform safety, loosening content moderation, and reinstating controversial accounts. These actions, they claim, alienated customers and disrupted X's business, leading to plummeting ad revenue.

The advertisers also contend that even if their actions were politically motivated, they are protected under the First Amendment and fall outside antitrust law. Additionally, some defendants, including WFA, CVS Health, and Nestlé, have sought dismissal on jurisdictional grounds, arguing the Texas court lacks authority over them, as reported by Reuters on May 15, 2025 ([Advertisers ask US judge to dismiss lawsuit by Musk’s X over ad spending](https://www.reuters.com/legal/litigation/advertisers-ask-us-judge-dismiss-lawsuit-by-musks-x-over-ad-spending-2025-05-15/)).

#### Recent Developments and Case Status
Several notable developments have occurred in 2025:
- In October 2024, X dropped Unilever from the lawsuit after reaching an agreement, with X committing to meet Unilever's responsibility standards for brand safety, as noted by Reuters on October 11, 2024 ([Elon Musk's X drops Unilever from advertiser boycott lawsuit](https://www.reuters.com/legal/elon-musks-x-drops-unilever-advertiser-boycott-lawsuit-2024-10-11/)).
- In May 2025, X dropped claims against Twitch "without prejudice," meaning it could potentially refile, after an undisclosed agreement condition was not met, according to Business Insider ([Advertisers hit back at Elon Musk's ad boycott lawsuit, saying X disrupted its own business and 'alienated' customers](https://www.businessinsider.com/advertisers-elon-musk-x-ad-boycott-lawsuit-dismiss-2025-5)).

As of June 2, 2025, the lawsuit remains ongoing, with no final resolution. Legal experts, as cited in Business Insider on August 8, 2024 ([Elon Musk Has a Weak Case Against Advertisers but That's Not the Point](https://www.businessinsider.com/elon-musk-x-advertiser-lawsuits-silence-opponents-2024-8)), suggest X's case may be weak due to the difficulty in proving collusion, but the litigation could pressure ad industry resources and chill future responsible media initiatives.

#### Impact and Broader Implications
The lawsuit has had significant repercussions beyond the legal arena. GARM, a small not-for-profit initiative, was discontinued in August 2024 due to the lawsuit's resource drain, as reported by CNN on August 9, 2024 ([Elon Musk’s X just sued a nonprofit advertising group out of existence](https://www.cnn.com/2024/08/09/tech/elon-musk-garm-advertisers-lawsuit/index.html)). WFA, however, remains operational and a defendant in the case. The lawsuit also coincides with political scrutiny, with the House Judiciary Committee, led by Jim Jordan, publishing a 2024 investigation alleging GARM colluded to boycott conservative media, as noted in Business Insider ([House Judiciary Committee probe into GARM brand safety and conservative media](https://www.businessinsider.com/house-judiciary-committee-probe-garm-brand-safety-conservative-media-2024-5)). This adds a layer of controversy, with X leveraging such reports to support its claims, though the investigation is separate from the lawsuit.

The lawsuit by Elon Musk's X against advertisers for the 2022 boycott is a complex legal battle, with X alleging illegal collusion and advertisers defending their individual business decisions. As of June 2, 2025, the case is unresolved, with significant legal and industry implications, including the dissolution of GARM and ongoing political scrutiny.
